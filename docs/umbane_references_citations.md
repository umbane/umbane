## Citation Usage Guide

### How Citations Are Referenced in the Paper

The paper uses **footnote-style citations** with bracketed superscript numbers, e.g. [^1], [^2], etc.

Each citation number corresponds to a source in this reference list, with specific page numbers or sections noted where applicable.

### Example Citation Format in Text:

**Correct**:
"According to the Carbon Knowledge Hub, 14.2 million carbon credits have been issued by South African projects as of June 2025[^1], with a single cookstove project accounting for 67% of 2024 supply[^1]."

**Correct**:
"The JSE Ventures Carbon Market launched on October 12, 2023[^9] in partnership with Xpansiv[^10], utilizing a white-label version of the CBL platform[^11]."

**Correct**:
"GCC, based in Sheffield, UK at 400 Springvale Road[^14], serves as the Central Issuer for I-REC in South Africa and over 20 other countries[^15]."

## Data Freshness and Limitations

### Data Currency
- Carbon market statistics: Current as of June 3, 2025 (Carbon Knowledge Hub data)[^1]
- Regulatory framework: Current as of March 2025 (Phase 2 proposals published)[^2][^7]
- JSE/Xpansiv partnership: Current as of October 2023 (launch date)[^9]
- I-REC issuer information: Current as of November 2023 (I-TRACK Foundation website)[^15]

### Known Data Gaps
1. **JSE Ventures Trading Volumes**: Official trading volume data not publicly available; estimates based on spot market observation and comparable exchanges
2. **I-REC Device Count in Cape Town**: Exact number (19 devices) needs verification from Evident Device Register
3. **Prosumer Solar Penetration**: 50,000 households with solar in Cape Town metro is estimate based on City of Cape Town Energy Directorate informal reports
4. **aC Token Pricing**: Projections (R150-R180/ton) based on Verra VCU spot prices and COAS trading activity; actual aC market pricing TBD post-launch

### Methodology for Unverifiable Claims
Where primary sources are unavailable, the paper uses:
1. **Conservative estimates** (e.g., lower bound of trading volume ranges)
2. **Analogous market data** (e.g., Toucan Protocol volumes as proxy for tokenized carbon markets)
3. **Explicit qualification** (e.g., "estimated", "projected", "based on pilot data")
